# Comparison Report - Event3

## Shared Facts

- Donald Trump is the US President
- There is a conflict involving Iran
- The conflict has caused a global energy shock
- The US economy is being affected by the conflict

## Unique Facts

**Article 1**
- The US-Israeli war in Iran has lasted more than six weeks
- The economy grew by 2% on an annualised basis in the first quarter of 2026
- The cost of living is a major concern for Americans
- The Nasdaq has gained around 10% since the start of the conflict

**Article 2**
- Trump has announced a tariff hike on cars and trucks from the European Union
- The tariff hike could cost Germany nearly $18 billion in output
- The German finance minister's chief adviser has advised caution towards Trump

**Article 3**
- Trump's approval rating on the economy has dropped to 30%
- The consumer price index climbed 3.3% in March from a year ago
- Only about half of Republicans approve of Trump's handling of the cost of living
- The AP-NORC poll found that about three-quarters of US adults described the US economy as 'very' or 'somewhat' poor

## Contradictions

- Article 1 states that the war has driven oil prices up, with a barrel of Brent crude hitting a four-year high of $126, while Article 3 quotes Trump as saying he was 'surprised' that oil prices were only around $90 a barrel
- Article 1 suggests that the economy is growing, while Article 3 states that about three-quarters of US adults described the US economy as 'very' or 'somewhat' poor

## Summary

The three articles discuss the impact of the US-Iran conflict on the US economy and President Trump's approval ratings. While Article 1 suggests that the economy is growing, Article 3 states that Trump's approval rating on the economy has dropped. Article 2 discusses the potential impact of Trump's tariff hike on the European Union. The articles highlight the contradictions and challenges faced by the US economy and the Trump administration.
