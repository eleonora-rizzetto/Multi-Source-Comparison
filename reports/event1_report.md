# Comparison Report - Event1

## Shared Facts

- Elon Musk and Sam Altman co-founded OpenAI
- OpenAI was initially a non-profit
- Musk and Altman are currently in a feud and are involved in a trial
- The trial is taking place in a federal courtroom in California
- Musk is suing OpenAI and its co-founder and president Greg Brockman, along with Microsoft
- OpenAI released ChatGPT in 2022
- Musk has started his own AI start-up, xAI

## Unique Facts

**Article 1**
- Musk called Altman 'Scam Altman' in a post on X
- Musk offered to purchase OpenAI's assets for $97.4bn
- OpenAI was valued at $157bn in a recent funding round
- Musk has donated around $40m to OpenAI
- Musk's use of ketamine will not be allowed in court

**Article 2**
- Musk testified for more than seven hours over three days
- Musk described OpenAI as a 'charity'
- Musk recruited Ilya Sutskever from Google
- Musk said OpenAI relied on his connections with Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella and Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang
- Musk learned from discussions with Larry Page that the Google founder lacked concern about AI safety

**Article 3**
- Tempers have flared on both sides of the high-stakes trial
- Judge Yvonne Gonzalez Rogers instructed the parties not to discuss the dangers of AI to humanity during the course of the trial
- Musk accused opposing attorney William Savitt of asking misleading questions
- Savitt asked Musk about earlier testimony where he said that as long as investor profits were capped, OpenAI wasn't in violation of agreements to keep it a nonprofit

## Contradictions

- Musk claims OpenAI was meant to be a charity, while OpenAI argues it was never promised to remain a nonprofit forever
- Musk says he was manipulated by OpenAI's founders, while OpenAI claims Musk is motivated by jealousy and regret for walking away from the company
- Musk argues that OpenAI's pivot to a for-profit venture was a betrayal, while OpenAI argues it was a necessary step to advance the company's mission

## Summary

Elon Musk and Sam Altman, co-founders of OpenAI, are involved in a high-stakes trial over the company's pivot from a non-profit to a for-profit venture, with Musk claiming he was betrayed and OpenAI arguing that Musk's lawsuit is an attempt to undercut their growth and bolster his own AI start-up, xAI.
