# Comparison Report - Event1

## Shared Facts

- Elon Musk and Sam Altman co-founded OpenAI as a nonprofit in 2015
- Musk is suing OpenAI, alleging that the company strayed from its core mission
- The trial is taking place in a federal courtroom in Oakland, California
- Musk and Altman will testify in the trial

## Unique Facts

**Article 1**
- Musk called Altman 'Scam Altman' in a post on X
- Musk is asking for billions of dollars in 'wrongful gains' to fund OpenAI's nonprofit arm
- OpenAI was valued at $157bn in a recent funding round and is approaching a rumored public listing with a value of about $850bn

**Article 2**
- Musk testified that OpenAI relied on his connections for top-tier talent and computing power
- Musk said he recruited Ilya Sutskever from Google while Larry Page and Sergei Brin tried to entice him to stay
- Musk said he asked Altman about an investment of $10 billion in OpenAI by Microsoft, which he described as a 'bait and switch'

**Article 3**
- Judge Yvonne Gonzalez Rogers told Musk's lawyers that the trial is not about the safety risks of artificial intelligence
- Musk accused opposing attorney William Savitt of asking misleading questions designed to trick him and the jury
- Savitt asked Musk why he hasn't started a nonprofit himself, eight years after he left OpenAI

## Contradictions

- Musk claims that OpenAI was supposed to remain a nonprofit, while OpenAI argues that there was no promise to remain a nonprofit forever
- Musk says he donated around $40m to OpenAI, while OpenAI contends that Musk is motivated by jealousy and regret for walking away from the company

## Summary

Elon Musk and Sam Altman, co-founders of OpenAI, are engaged in a high-stakes trial over the company's pivot from nonprofit to for-profit status, with Musk alleging that OpenAI strayed from its core mission and OpenAI arguing that Musk's lawsuit is aimed at undercutting its rapid growth and bolstering his own AI company, xAI.
